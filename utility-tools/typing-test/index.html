Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about typing speed tests

The average typing speed for adults is around 40 WPM. A speed of 60–80 WPM is considered good for professional use. Touch typists and professional typists often exceed 80–100 WPM, while the world record exceeds 200 WPM.

WPM (Words Per Minute) measures how many five-character groups you type per minute, regardless of actual word length. CPM (Characters Per Minute) counts the total number of individual characters typed per minute. CPM is a more precise raw measure; WPM is the standard benchmark for comparing typists.

Typing accuracy is the percentage of characters typed correctly out of all characters typed. For example, if you type 200 characters and make 4 errors, your accuracy is (196/200) × 100 = 98%. High accuracy is just as important as high speed for productive typing.

Gross WPM is calculated by dividing the total number of correctly typed characters by 5 (one standard word = 5 characters), then dividing by the number of minutes elapsed. This tool calculates net WPM using only correct characters so your accuracy is always reflected in the score.

Consistent daily practice is the most effective way to improve. Focus on accuracy before speed, learn proper finger positioning on the home row keys (ASDF JKL;), avoid looking at the keyboard, and use touch typing technique. Regular tests with this tool help you track your progress over time.
