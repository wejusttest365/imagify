FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions About Typing Tests

Everything you need to know about measuring and improving your typing speed.

What is a good typing speed in WPM?

The average adult types around 40 WPM. A speed of 60–80 WPM is considered good for professional use. Touch typists often exceed 80–100 WPM, while competitive typists and the world record exceed 200 WPM.

What is the difference between WPM and CPM?

WPM (Words Per Minute) measures five-character groups per minute regardless of actual word length. CPM (Characters Per Minute) counts every individual character. CPM is the raw measure; WPM is the standard benchmark used to compare typists across the industry.

How is typing accuracy calculated?

Accuracy is the percentage of characters typed correctly out of all characters typed. For example, typing 200 characters with 4 errors gives (196÷200)×100 = 98% accuracy. High accuracy matters as much as high speed for productive typing.

Is my typing data stored anywhere?

No. Everything runs locally in your browser using JavaScript. No keystrokes, scores, or personal data are ever sent to or stored on any server. Your privacy is completely protected.

How is WPM calculated in this test?

WPM is calculated by dividing correctly typed characters by 5 (one standard word = 5 characters), then dividing by the number of minutes elapsed. Only correct characters count, so accuracy is always reflected in your WPM score.

How can I improve my typing speed?

Focus on accuracy before speed. Learn proper finger positioning on the home row (ASDF JKL;), avoid looking at the keyboard, and practise consistently every day. Regular testing with this tool helps you track measurable progress over time.

What difficulty levels are available?

There are three difficulty levels. Easy uses short common words ideal for beginners. Medium uses everyday sentence-level prose for intermediate typists. Hard uses advanced vocabulary, punctuation and complex sentence structures to challenge experienced typists.
