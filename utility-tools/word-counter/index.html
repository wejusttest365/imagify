Readability Guide

Flesch Reading Ease Score Explained

The Flesch Reading Ease formula measures how easy your text is to read based on sentence length and syllables per word. It's the most widely used readability metric in publishing and education.

Score Range Reading Level Typical Audience Best For Verdict
90–100 5th grade 10–11 year olds Children's content, simple instructions, FAQs ✅ Very Easy
80–90 6th grade General public Conversational blog posts, chat messages, casual emails ✅ Easy
70–80 7th grade Teenagers & adults News articles, product descriptions, web copy ✅ Fairly Easy
60–70 8th–9th grade General adult audience Standard web content — the target for most marketing & SEO writing ⚠️ Standard
50–60 10th–12th grade High school graduates Technical content, corporate communications, longer-form journalism ⚠️ Fairly Difficult
30–50 College level University-educated adults Academic papers, legal documents, scientific reports 🟠 Difficult
0–30 Professional Domain experts only Legal contracts, medical literature, technical specifications ❌ Very Difficult
SEO Writing

Keyword Density: How to Optimise Without Over-Optimising

Keyword density measures how frequently your target keyword appears in a piece of content. Modern SEO rewards natural, contextual usage — not repetition.

DensityAssessmentSEO ImpactRecommendation
<0.5% Under-optimised May not rank for target term Add natural keyword mentions; use LSI variants and related terms
0.5–1% Light Acceptable, depends on competition Consider adding keyword in headings, introduction, and conclusion
1–2% Ideal ✅ Best for most content Maintain natural flow; include semantic variants and synonyms
2–4% High Risk of over-optimisation Replace some exact matches with synonyms; check for natural readability
>4% Keyword stuffing ❌ Google penalty risk Significantly reduce density; prioritise reader experience over repetition
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions About Word Counting

Everything writers, students, bloggers, and marketers ask about word and character counting.

How do you count words in a text?

Words are counted by splitting the text on whitespace (spaces, tabs, line breaks) and counting non-empty segments. Hyphenated words like "well-known" count as one word. Punctuation attached to words (like the full stop in "word.") is stripped before counting. This tool updates the word count in real time as you type or paste.

What is the difference between characters with and without spaces?

Characters with spaces counts every character including spaces, tabs, and newlines. Characters without spaces counts only non-whitespace characters — letters, numbers, and punctuation marks. Academic journals, legal contracts, and some content platforms use the character-without-spaces count as the official length measurement.

How is reading time calculated?

Reading time is estimated by dividing the total word count by 200 — the average adult reading speed in words per minute (wpm). Speaking time uses 130 wpm, a comfortable clear public-speaking pace. Both are approximations; actual time varies with text complexity, individual reader speed, and speaking style.

What word count should a blog post be for SEO?

Most SEO practitioners recommend 1,500–2,500 words for competitive blog posts targeting search traffic. In-depth guides and pillar pages often exceed 3,000 words. However, word count alone does not determine ranking — quality, relevance, and topical depth matter more. Short posts of 500–800 words can rank well for conversational, low-competition queries. Always match content length to what the searcher's intent actually requires.

What is keyword density and what is the ideal percentage?

Keyword density is the percentage of times a specific keyword appears relative to total word count: (occurrences ÷ total words) × 100. The recommended SEO range is 1–2% for primary keywords. Exceeding 4% risks being flagged as keyword stuffing by search engines, which can lower rankings. This tool shows density automatically for your top words.

What is the Flesch Reading Ease score?

The Flesch Reading Ease score rates text readability on a 0–100 scale. It is calculated from average sentence length and average syllables per word. A score of 90–100 is very easy (5th grade level); 60–70 is standard (8th–9th grade); 30–50 is difficult (college level); below 30 is very difficult (professional). Most general web content should target a score above 60.

How many words is a standard page?

A standard double-spaced page in 12pt Times New Roman is approximately 250–300 words. A single-spaced page is approximately 500–600 words. An A4 Word document page averages around 500 words depending on margins and line spacing. A 5-minute speech delivered at a normal pace is approximately 650–700 words.

Is my text stored or sent to a server?

No. All analysis runs entirely inside your browser using JavaScript. None of your text, content, or data is sent to any server or stored anywhere. Everything is processed locally on your device and disappears when you close or refresh the page. This tool is 100% private and completely offline-capable once loaded.
