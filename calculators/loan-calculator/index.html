Loan Calculator vs. EMI Calculator

Both use the same formula — here's how they differ in scope

EMI Calculator
  • Focused purely on computing the fixed monthly installment amount.
  • Typically used once a specific principal, rate and tenure are already decided.
  • Best for a quick "what will my monthly payment be" check.
Loan Calculator
  • Shows EMI plus the full cost picture: total interest and total repayment.
  • Useful for comparing different loan offers, tenures or interest rates side by side.
  • Better suited for loan planning across personal, home, car or business loans.

The underlying math — the reducing-balance EMI formula — is identical in both tools. The difference is purely in what's displayed and how the tool is typically used.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about loan EMI calculation

Loan EMI is calculated using the formula EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / [(1+r)^n − 1], where P is the loan principal, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12 and by 100), and n is the total number of monthly installments.

An EMI calculator and a loan calculator generally use the same underlying formula to compute the monthly installment. A loan calculator is broader, additionally showing total interest payable and total repayment across the full loan tenure, useful for comparing different loan types such as personal, home, car or business loans.
