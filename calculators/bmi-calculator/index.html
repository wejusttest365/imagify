BMI Categories Chart

Standard WHO BMI classification for adults

BMI Range Category General Health Note
Below 18.5 Underweight May indicate insufficient nutrition; consult a healthcare provider
18.5 – 24.9 Normal weight Generally considered a healthy weight range for most adults
25.0 – 29.9 Overweight Increased risk for certain health conditions; lifestyle review advised
30.0 – 34.9 Obese (Class I) Higher health risk; professional guidance recommended
35.0 and above Obese (Class II & III) Significant health risk; medical consultation strongly advised

BMI is a general screening tool and does not account for muscle mass, bone density, age, sex, or ethnicity. Always consult a healthcare professional for a complete health assessment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about BMI calculation

BMI is calculated by dividing weight in kilograms by the square of height in meters: BMI = weight (kg) / [height (m)]². For imperial units, BMI = 703 × weight (lb) / [height (in)]².

According to WHO classification, a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is generally considered a healthy weight range for most adults. Below 18.5 is underweight, 25 to 29.9 is overweight, and 30 or above is classified as obese.

BMI is a useful general screening tool but does not directly measure body fat or account for muscle mass, bone density, age, or sex. Athletes and very muscular individuals may have a high BMI without excess body fat, so BMI should be considered alongside other health indicators.
