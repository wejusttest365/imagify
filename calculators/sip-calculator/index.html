How SIP Returns Are Calculated

Understanding the formula behind your SIP maturity value

Term Meaning Example
P Monthly SIP investment amount ₹5,000 per month
i Monthly rate of return (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100) 12% p.a. → 1% monthly
n Total number of monthly installments 10 years → 120 months
M Maturity value = P × [(1+i)ⁿ – 1] / i × (1+i) Total value at end of tenure

Returns shown are estimates based on the expected rate of return you enter. Actual mutual fund returns are market-linked, vary over time, and are never guaranteed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about SIP calculation

SIP maturity value is calculated using the formula M = P × ({[1 + i]^n – 1} / i) × (1 + i), where P is the monthly investment amount, i is the monthly rate of return (annual rate divided by 12 and by 100), and n is the total number of monthly installments.

Invested amount is the total of all monthly contributions you make over the SIP tenure (monthly amount multiplied by number of months). Estimated returns is the wealth gained on top of that, calculated as the maturity value minus the total invested amount.

Most equity mutual fund SIP calculators use a long-term average return assumption between 10% and 15% per annum, though actual returns depend on market performance and the specific fund chosen and are never guaranteed.
