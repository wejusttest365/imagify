What Is a Strong Password?

A strong password is a sequence of characters that is difficult for a human or a computer to guess. Attackers use automated techniques such as brute-force attacks and credential stuffing to test millions of combinations every second, so predictable passwords are broken far faster than most people expect.

Strong passwords typically:

  • Are at least 12–16 characters long
  • Mix uppercase and lowercase letters
  • Include numbers and symbols
  • Avoid real words, names, or birthdays
  • Aren't reused across multiple accounts

Short, predictable passwords like "password123" or a pet's name can be guessed in seconds. A long, random password generated by a computer is far harder to crack because there's no pattern for an attacker to exploit.

  • Pairing with other character types for variety

Numbers (0–9)

Increases unpredictability and total possible combinations.

Used for:

Meeting minimum complexity requirements

Adding extra entropy without extra length

Symbols (!@#$%^&*)

The strongest addition for resisting brute-force attacks.

Typically required by:

  • Banking and financial websites

Tips for Creating and Storing Strong Passwords

For better account security:

  • Use a unique password for every account.
  • Store passwords in a dedicated password manager rather than a sticky note or plain text file.
  • Enable two-factor authentication wherever it's offered.
  • Never share your password over email or chat.
  • Change a password immediately if a service reports a data breach.
  • Avoid using personal information like birthdays or pet names.
