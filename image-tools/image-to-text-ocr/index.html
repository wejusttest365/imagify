Tips for Best Results

How to Get the Most Accurate OCR Results

Image quality directly affects OCR accuracy. Follow these guidelines for the cleanest text extraction.

Factor Recommendation Impact on Accuracy Notes
Resolution Use high-resolution images (300 DPI or higher) 🟢 Very High Blurry or low-res images cause the most errors
Contrast Dark text on a light background works best 🟢 Very High Avoid images where text blends into the background
Rotation Keep text horizontally aligned 🟡 High Tilted or rotated text reduces recognition accuracy
Font Standard printed fonts give the best results 🟡 Medium Decorative, cursive, or stylised fonts may be misread
File Format PNG preferred; JPG also works well 🟡 Medium PNG avoids compression artefacts that can distort characters
Language English text is recognised most accurately 🟠 Variable This tool uses the English Tesseract language model
FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions About Image to Text OCR

Everything you need to know about extracting text from images with WebToolOcean's free OCR tool, including how it works, supported formats, accuracy, and privacy.

What is an OCR tool?

OCR stands for Optical Character Recognition. It's a technology that scans an image, identifies characters and words within it, and converts them into machine-readable, editable text that can be copied, searched, and edited. Our tool uses Tesseract, one of the most accurate open-source OCR engines available, running entirely in your browser using JavaScript and WebAssembly technology.

Is my image uploaded to a server?

No. All OCR processing happens entirely inside your browser using JavaScript and WebAssembly technology. Your images are never sent to or stored on any external server or cloud service. Your files remain on your device at all times, completely private and secure. This also means the tool works offline.

What image formats does this tool support?

The tool accepts any image format your browser supports, including JPG, JPEG, PNG, WebP, GIF, and BMP. For best OCR accuracy and text recognition, we recommend using PNG format or high-quality JPG files with clear, sharp, readable text and good contrast.

Why is the extracted text inaccurate?

OCR accuracy depends heavily on image quality and text characteristics. Common causes of recognition errors include: low resolution or blurry images, poor contrast between text and background, heavily stylised, cursive, or decorative fonts, skewed or rotated text in the image, or extremely small text. For best results, use high-resolution (300 DPI or higher) images with standard printed fonts and strong contrast between text and background.

Does this tool work on mobile?

Yes. The tool is fully responsive and works on iOS and Android mobile devices. You can upload images directly from your phone's camera roll, take a new photo using your device camera, or select images from cloud storage. OCR processing and text extraction work seamlessly on mobile browsers. Note that OCR processing may be slightly slower on some mobile devices depending on available memory.

Can it extract text from handwritten notes?

It can, with varying accuracy depending on handwriting quality. Tesseract OCR performs best on printed and typed text. Clear, neatly written block letters will extract reasonably well with good accuracy, but cursive, connected, or messy handwriting may produce errors and misrecognitions. For best handwriting recognition results, ensure the image is high resolution, well-lit, and has strong contrast.

What languages does the OCR support?

This tool currently uses the English language model for Tesseract OCR. It works best on English text and Latin-script languages. However, accuracy for non-English or non-Latin scripts such as Arabic, Chinese, Japanese, Hindi, Russian Cyrillic, or Greek will be limited. For multilingual support, consider uploading to a specialized multilingual OCR service.

Can I extract text from a PDF?

This tool works with image files only, not PDFs directly. However, if you have a scanned PDF (where the PDF contains images of pages), you can take a screenshot of each page and upload those image files to extract text. For text-based PDFs that contain selectable text, you can copy text directly in any PDF reader or viewer without needing OCR technology.

Is this tool completely free?

Yes, this tool is 100% completely free with no hidden charges, no usage limits, no watermarks, no file size restrictions, and no account registration required. You can extract text from as many images as you want, whenever you want, completely free forever.

Do I need to install anything?

No installation is required. The tool runs entirely in your web browser using JavaScript and WebAssembly technology. No plugins, extensions, browser add-ons, or software downloads are needed. It works on any modern browser including Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge, and Apple Safari.
