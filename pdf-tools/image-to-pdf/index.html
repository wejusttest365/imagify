FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions About Image to PDF

Everything you need to know about converting images to PDF with WebToolOcean.

What image formats can I convert to PDF?

This tool supports JPG/JPEG, PNG, and WEBP image files. You can mix formats freely — for example, combine JPG photos and PNG screenshots into the same PDF.

Are my images uploaded to a server?

No. All conversion happens entirely inside your browser using JavaScript. Your images are never sent to or stored on any server — everything stays on your device.

Can I combine multiple images into one PDF?

Yes. Upload as many images as you like and they'll each become a page in a single PDF, in the order you arrange them.

Can I control the page size and orientation?

Yes. Choose between A4, Letter, or "Fit to Image" page sizing, set orientation to portrait, landscape, or auto-detect, and adjust margins before converting.

Does this tool work on mobile?

Yes, the tool is fully responsive and works on iOS and Android. You can pick photos straight from your camera roll, and converting and downloading both work in mobile browsers.

Can I reorder images before converting?

Yes. Drag and drop your uploaded images into any order, or sort them alphabetically with one click. The final PDF will follow that exact page order.

Will converting reduce my image quality?

No meaningful quality is lost. Your original image resolution is preserved and embedded into the PDF page, so photos and scans stay sharp and clear.

Is there a limit on how many images I can add?

There's no hard limit — add as many images as your browser can handle. For very large batches (35+ high-resolution photos), conversion may simply take a little longer.

Is this tool completely free?

Yes, this tool is 100% free with no hidden charges, no watermarks, no account registration, and no file count limits — forever.

Do I need to install anything?

No installation is required. The tool runs entirely in your web browser using JavaScript — no plugins, extensions, or software downloads needed.
