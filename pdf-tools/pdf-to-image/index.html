FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions About PDF to Image

Everything you need to know about converting PDF pages to images with WebToolOcean.

What image formats can I convert my PDF pages to?

You can export pages as PNG (lossless, best for text and diagrams) or JPG (smaller file size, best for photo-heavy pages). Every page in the PDF is converted using the format you choose.

Is my PDF uploaded to a server?

No. The entire conversion happens locally in your browser using JavaScript. Your PDF and the resulting images never leave your device or get sent to any server.

Can I convert every page at once?

Yes. Upload your PDF and click "Convert Pages" — every page is rendered as an image automatically. You can then download them individually or all together as a single ZIP file.

Can I control the image resolution?

Yes. Choose 1x for standard resolution, 2x for high quality, or 3x for maximum sharpness — useful when printing or zooming into fine details.

Does this tool work on mobile?

Yes, the tool is fully responsive and works on iOS and Android. You can upload a PDF from your device storage or cloud drive, and conversion and downloads both work in mobile browsers.

Will the images look exactly like the PDF pages?

Yes. Each page is rendered exactly as it appears in the PDF, including text, fonts, images, and layout, then captured as a flat image at the resolution you select.

Can I convert password-protected PDFs?

The tool cannot open password-protected or encrypted PDFs. You'll need to remove the password first using your PDF reader, then upload the unlocked file here.

Is there a limit on the number of pages?

There's no hard page limit — the tool converts every page in your PDF. For very long documents at high resolution, conversion may simply take a little longer to complete.

Is this tool completely free?

Yes, this tool is 100% free with no hidden charges, no watermarks, no account registration, and no page limits — forever.

Do I need to install anything?

No installation is required. The tool runs entirely in your web browser using JavaScript — no plugins, extensions, or software downloads needed.
