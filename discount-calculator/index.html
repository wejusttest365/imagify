Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about discount calculation

A discount is calculated by applying the discount percentage to the original price. Discount amount = (Original price × Discount %) / 100, and Final price = Original price − Discount amount.

Discount percentage = [(Original price − Final price) / Original price] × 100. This tells you what percentage off the original price you actually paid.

Stacked discounts are applied sequentially, not added together. The first discount is applied to the original price, and the second discount is then applied to the already-discounted price, not the original price.

A flat discount subtracts a fixed amount from the price regardless of the price itself, while a percentage discount subtracts a proportion of the price, so the actual amount saved varies with the original price.
